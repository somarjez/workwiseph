# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primarily people evaluating the work — recruiters, hiring managers, and clients reviewing
this as a portfolio piece — who should immediately read it as a credible, real analytics
product. Secondarily, anyone interested in Philippine labor trends (students, analysts)
who wants to read the data. Context: desktop browser, scanning quickly, forming a judgment
about quality and rigor within seconds, then drilling into specific indicators.

## Product Purpose

Turn 20+ years of PSA Labor Force Survey tables into an interactive, trustworthy analytics
dashboard covering employment, underemployment, industry, education, pay, working hours,
and short-term forecasts. Success = a visitor trusts the numbers, finds an insight in under
a minute, and comes away believing the maker can ship a polished, end-to-end data product.

## Brand Personality

Editorial, authoritative, quietly confident — the feel of a well-designed statistics
report or a data-journalism feature, not a generic SaaS dashboard. Three words:
considered, credible, legible. The interface should feel like it was art-directed.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS-analytics templates: blue-gradient hero, identical icon-cards, rounded-blob
  illustrations, "Supercharge your insights" copy.
- Cluttered BI tools (stock Power BI / default Tableau) with chart-junk and heavy chrome.
- AI-slop tells: tiny tracked uppercase eyebrows on every section, gradient text, glassmorphism,
  cream/sand "warm minimal" body backgrounds, the big-number-with-gradient hero-metric template.

## Design Principles

1. **The data is the hero.** Chrome recedes; charts and numbers get the contrast and space.
2. **Publication-grade typography.** A serif display voice for titles carries the editorial
   identity; everything functional stays in a clean, legible sans.
3. **Restraint with one confident accent.** Mostly ink-on-paper; a single accent marks
   actions, current state, and the active data series — never decoration.
4. **Earned familiarity.** Standard, trustworthy affordances (nav, tables, controls); the
   tool disappears into the task. Distinctiveness comes from type and space, not invented widgets.
5. **Every state designed.** Loading (skeletons), empty (teaches), error (honest) on every surface.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥ 4.5:1, large text ≥ 3:1, visible focus rings, full keyboard operability.
Light default with a dark toggle; both themes meet contrast. All motion has a
`prefers-reduced-motion` fallback. Charts pair color with labels/position so meaning never
relies on hue alone.
